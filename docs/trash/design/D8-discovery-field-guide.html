01

The Artifact Hunt (What to Collect)

Do not just ask how it works; get the physical proof. Since you have access to computers and file cabinets, collect samples of everything.

Blank & Filled Report Templates Collect blank templates AND filled samples of every log, quality sheet, and register used on the pilot line. [RAIS Critical]
Historical Excel Dumps Ask or copy 3 to 5 historical Excel files for each report type. The more historical rows, the better we can train the reconciliation algorithm. [RAIS Critical]
Official Factory Org Chart Get the org chart containing exact job designations, reporting chains, and names for RBAC planning. [MO!D]
Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) Obtain copy of SOPs regarding line inspections, defect logging, and material handoff. [MO!D]
GM Upward Performance Reports Get a blank/filled copy of the report the GM sends upward to executive management. Our system must output matching tables. [RAIS Core]
Monthly Quality Review Output Grab the slide pack, minutes, or Excel dashboards created for the last monthly quality and rejection review meeting. [RAIS Core]
Cost & Price Allocation Sheets If allowed, get the rupee cost per unit at each process stage, including scrap cost and rework cost parameters. [MO!D ROI]
Mission 1 Field Notes
02

Floor Walk & Observations

Actually walking the line is how you spot reality. Walk stage-by-stage and record how people do their jobs.

Trace the Physical Flow Walk the pilot line from raw material receiving, through assembly, molding, QC inspection, and sterilization, to shipping. [RAIS VSM Backbone]
Watch Data Recording Live Sit next to an operator at a station for 10 minutes. Watch them inspect parts and physically log readings. Note if they do it live or batch it at the end of the shift. [RAIS Ingestion]
Photograph Paper Logs & Whiteboards Photograph physical paper logs, quality whiteboards, performance charts, and defect inspection areas (limit tags, sample boards). [RAIS Proof]
Observe Inter-Stage buffers (WIP) Look at where boxes/trolleys sit between molding and assembly. Are there labels? How is this buffer quantity recorded? [MO!D WIP]

🇮🇳 Shopfloor Jargon Primer (Helpful Vocabulary)

Use these terms to speak their language. Avoid technical English jargon like "Variance" or "Yield Loss" when talking to operators.

Rejections / Scrap

कटिंग / वेस्टेज / रिजेक्ट माल (Cutting/Wastage)

Material discarded due to errors.
Rework

रीवर्क / दोबारा ठीक करना (Rework)

Fixing an item so it passes checks.
Inspection / QC

चेकिंग / जांच (Checking)

Verifying items against quality SOPs.
Defect

कमी / खराबी / डिफेक्ट (Defect)

A specific flaw (bubble, scratch, leak).
04

Integrity Auditing (Find Excel Errors)

To prove the value of our software, we must show the GM where their current manual files are failing. Check the following specific locations for errors:

Audit: Assembly Yearly Sheet Formulas Check the total and average cells in their yearly sheets. Are they mathematically incorrect (e.g. adding percentages instead of calculating weighted values)? [RAIS Proof]
Audit: Missing Months (January Sheet) Look at the assembly sheets from January. Are they blank or half-empty, and does this lack of data distort the annual averages? [RAIS Proof]
Audit: Shopfloor Column Omissions Check if the summary totals at the bottom of the spreadsheets omit certain inspection columns by mistake. [RAIS Proof]
Audit: Total Rejections ≠ Sum of Reasons Sum up the counts of specific defect reasons (BM, PS, etc.) and see if they actually match the stated total rejection count column. [RAIS Proof]
